Timeslip

Nara triple-checked the coordinates on her digital uplink as the quantum accelerator thrummed with power. One chance to pull this off - to finally unravel the mysteries of time travel. Taking a steadying breath, she activated the sequence.

Blinding turquoise light engulfed the chamber as space-time distorted around her. For a fleeting moment, the very fabric of existence seemed to shift, stretch, twist. Nara's heart leapt into her throat as she glimpsed a vista of bizarre alien skyscrapers through a shimmering rift, their towering spires scraping an burnt-orange sky.

Then, with a deafening boom, she was violently hurled forward - or was it backward? Gasping for breath in pitch darkness, Nara fumbled for a light. By its dim glow, her eyes widened in shock - she appeared to be trapped in some sort of primitive cave dwelling. Archaic etchings covered the walls, depicted crude stick figures in menacing circles.

Impossible...unless...had she actually traveled back to mankind's ancient past? A primal roar echoed through the shadows, inhuman footsteps drawing near. Nara froze, scarcely able to process the mind-bending truth - she was tens of thousands of years away from home with absolutely no way back.
