Echoes of Eliza

A cold fog crept through the quiet village as the old clock tower struck midnight. Sarah jolted awake, her heart racing. The music box on her nightstand was playing softly - a tinkling lullaby she hadn't heard since childhood. But she lived alone, and the box had been broken for years.

As the melody faded, Sarah noticed a small key resting atop the box that hadn't been there before. With trembling fingers, she picked it up. It fit perfectly in the locket around her neck - a locket she had never been able to open.

Sarah hesitated, her fingers hovering over the locket. The key seemed to pulse with an otherworldly energy. Taking a deep breath, she inserted it into the tiny keyhole and turned.

The locket sprang open with a soft click. Inside was a faded photograph of a woman who looked remarkably like Sarah, holding a baby. On the back, in faded script, were the words: "To protect you always, my dear Eliza." But Sarah's name wasn't Eliza.

"The music box began to play again, louder this time. Sarah whirled around, her heart pounding. The mirror on her dresser rippled like water, and through it, she could see a shadowy figure reaching out

A whisper filled the room, "It's time to come home, Eliza." Sarah stumbled back, clutching the locket. The room began to spin, the fog from outside seeping under her door. As the mist enveloped her, Sarah felt herself being pulled towards the mirror.

Just as her fingertips touched the glass, everything went black. When the sun rose the next morning, Sarah's room was empty. The music box sat silent, the key and locket nowhere to be found. Only a single line was written in the dust on the mirror: "The truth lies beyond the veil."
