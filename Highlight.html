Life of Med School Through The Glasses

It is at 7:37 am on a rainy morning of second tuesday of September in 2018 that she thinks " did I make a mistake choosing medical field?." This is her first time thinking like this. ( Little does she know it is the first time for an upcoming zilionth times! )

For someone who is first one in her whole family tree to come in this field and whose only exposure to the life of a doctor is through some cheesy Indian daily soap, Dill Mill Gayye, reality is less pretty , less pink and less presence of Dr. Armaan Malik. The last part is most depressing and she spends 3 days grieving about how cruel cable channels has lied to her. Don't take her wrong, she doesn't expect some la la land where patients are treated with pixie dust and doctors spend their time by singing around Disney musicals but excuse her a lil bit if the long gloomy corridors of hospital, cries of people from the postmortem house when they come to take the body and the mortuary just behind her hostel building do not seem very awe inspiring to her. Isn't it what she always dreams of?? Well let's have a glance at it..

The life in hostel is whole another story for her. She remembers watching and loving those high school chick flicks where the female lead goes through a full makeover and suddenly the nerdy braces wearing boring girl takes 180¬∞ turn and becomes someone straight out of Vogue cover. Well here she too gets her dream chance for makeover...But it's in reverse way. It involves less high fashion clothing and more salwar suits and an amount of hair oil which she's is sure will make her mom shed two tears of happiness.There are other rules also. Some she finds logical, some she finds funny and some she finds completely absurd. "I am supposed to remember all my Senior's name with their faces but I am not allowed to look at them.Also I would b honoured with the so called tag of the "Taazgi"(fresher) of the year if I could rattaficate all those couple's name just in the same way I used to pronounce the tables as a toddler in the kinder garden.. Does nobody think this is slightly absurd ?", she thinks frustratingly. She is told that all these rules are to strengthen the bond between juniors and seniors and only through utter determination, she barely avoids rolling her eyes. 1st year medicos are allowed to use hindi only but she ends up finding the whole thing quite informative and educational as she gets an opportunity to enrich her vocabulary with new words like "chur" ( crush/love) and kharab-pudina (badminton) and learn how to translate a full english rap song into hindi.Well there's a whole verse on that.. Just like the chronicles of Ramayana (the bal kand, aranya kand) there are many such kands... Will come to all these sooner or later ...

Classes start and she finds that college life is pretty similar to school life, except the part where everything is 10 times more difficult and her comprehending skill getting inversely proportional. Everyday, from 9 a.m to 5 p.m, she spends her time struggling through the effort of not zoning out during lectures, gathering enough courage to "stab" her own finger with a needle for hematology practical (medicos take "gave my sweat and blood" too literally) and making mnemonics for the mnemonics of the mnemonics for the actual answer. Surely she is learning but still there's sense of being lost. Learning so much of vocabulary she sometimes wonder " one day it will be so extensive that my cervical vertebrae ,nucleus pulposes and annulus fibroses would no longer be strong enough to lift this huge burden"..

She is in her first histology practical class and after squinting her eyes through the microscope for nearly 15 minutes , when she can't make a single sense out of it except that it is pink, she turns to her classmate and asks "which part of slide should I actually look at?". " Uhmm... maybe the pink part. " The answer of course does not help her with histology but she smiles realizing that everyone is equally lost as her. Talk about small mercies!

Now the infamous dissection class turns out to be everything and more. It's gross, it's terrifying but oddly fascinating. She feels that she is justified in her obvious hesitation in peeling down the skin from cadaver when she hasn't even cut a chicken in her life. Ah!! that night is more horrifying to her than a nightmare could ever be ..That old man lying in some sort of uneasy posture soaked in the formalin does not get out of her head.A point also transpires when her hands start shaking even with the thought of scalpel and the forceps .It is then when her ethics and morals came to her aid ."Our perception towards the things has the power to change anything" as mentioned by her mother .. Aah!! Their values and etiquettes always come by our side in the worst of our times.. And of course she is doing this in the greater welfare of the mankind.. Doesn't she?? After 2 weeks when she finds her hands and even face deep into the cavity of thorax of a cadaver, trying to find a nerve, it hits her that actually everything is not that bad. Barring the smell of formalin . It remains bad.

As time passes, things do not become easier but it becomes easier to handle things. Friends help too. Friends help a lot. From blowing up test tubes in biochemistry lab to saving "safest" seats in physiology tutorial classes where u could find a good network access to lending a shoulder to cry when homesickness becomes too much to handle. Friends always help.

She learns that medicos live in two extreme phase. Either they are busy making heart eyes at the pages of Grey's Anatomy with kind of passion which can give Peter Kavinsky a run for his money or partying like there's no tomorrow. There's no middle ground. They treat agreement for a mass bunk like a blood pact and couples are made with the clear intention of getting married and nothing less. She is 90% sure that the couple sitting in bench next to her has already decided on which coaching center they'll send their future doctor kids to. In conclusion medicos are a bunch of strange weirdos with a very concerning obsession with attendance and stethoscope so much that they could even perform their "varmala" with the stethoscope..

The leaves on the trees in front of her dorm window are back in the same color as in the start of session and with that it brings the first professional exams. She goes through her written exams in an okay-ish way. She is mostly relieved that her 2 a.m library trips with her friends bitching about "Preeti sikka" for her dormancy do not turn out to be for nothing. Viva and practical exam though is everything a medico's nightmare is made of. After her first viva she is convinced that the external professor used to probably give Raghu and Rajiv free tutions for Roadies audition. But guess the grilling is justified considering the stakes.

Finally it all ends.. Don't take it too serious as the other chronicles r yet to be explored and now as she waits for her result in her room in her home , she looks back to everything. One full year and now she fully understands it's not a walk on cake. But for all the issues, complains and whining, she can't think of a version of herself where she gives up on it..Inspite of all these still like a trainee hero she is destined to save lives and you know that's make her absolutely awesome..
